Pasta Salad

Pasta salad has always came in 2 ways: Italian pasta dishes and European cold salad dishes. Cold pasta dishes have been in Italy since the Renissance. This salad often included oil, vinegar, and herbs.The modern version of the pasta became popular in th US in the 20th century. As pasta became more available, home cooks begain mixing chilled noodles that were cooked with vegetables, dressings, and meats.By the 60s and 70s pasta was popular at deli counters, and mainly with the growth of convenience foods and mayonnaise based salads like egg salad and pasta salads. Today, pasta salad has many different types around the world, but its core idea, a chilled pasta dish that has many flavors, remains similar to the early Italian inspirations and American creativity.

  • Ingredients

  • 1 pound of rontini pasta, uncooked
  • 8 ounces of fresh mozzerella cheese pearls
  • 8 ounces of salami and pepperoni
  • 6 ounces black olives pitted, sliced in halves
  • 1/2 red onion, diced
  • 1 1/2 cups of cherry tomatos in halves
  • 2 tablespoons chopped parsly
  • 1/2 cup of freshly grated parmesan cheese

  • Salad Dressing Ingredients

  • Make the salad dressing by combining all ingrediants into a bowl or cup.
  • Cook Pasta for 7-12 minutes. Drain the pasta and then rinse the pasta with cold water. Allow it to cool for 25 minutes.
  • Combine: add pasta to a large bowl and pour half of the salad dressing over it. Toss to combine. Add remaining ingrredients and dress and toss everything to combine.
  • Refridgerate: Cover and Refridgerate for 1 hour or long, before serving.
